# Output Format Specifications

Each section below defines the content structure and Word doc formatting for one output type.
Use the shared style constants from SKILL.md (ACCENT, DARK, LIGHT_BG, etc.) throughout.

---

## 1. Industry Overview

**Subtitle line:** "Industry Overview for AP Automation Sales"
**Header:** "INDUSTRY OVERVIEW | [Industry Name] | Vic.ai Sales Intelligence"

### Sections (in order):

**Sector at a Glance**
A 2-sentence description of the industry, then a 2-column table with rows:
- Global Market Size
- Key Segments
- Regulatory Environment
- End Markets Served
- Business Model (how companies in this sector sell)
- Ownership Profile (PE-backed, family-owned, public mix)

**How AP & Finance Teams Are Structured**
- Centralized vs. Decentralized AP (prose, 3–4 sentences — common patterns in this sector)
- Finance Org Chart table: Role | Responsibilities & AP Relevance
  Include: CFO/VP Finance, Controller, AP Manager, Procurement Manager, IT/ERP Manager, Shared Services (if relevant)
- How Decisions Get Made (3–4 bullet points specific to this sector's ownership norms)

**Key Terms & Industry Language**
One consolidated term table (Term | Definition | Why It Matters for AP/Finance).
Include 6–8 terms that are **unique or specific to this industry's AP and finance operations** — terms a Vic.ai rep would hear from a CFO, Controller, or AP Manager in this sector that they wouldn't commonly encounter in other industries. Skip generic accounting or AP terms (invoice, PO, 3-way match, DPO) unless the industry has a sector-specific meaning or twist. Focus on operational and billing language specific to this sector.

**Why AP/Finance is Uniquely Complex in This Industry**
Intro sentence, then 4–5 numbered sub-headings each with a paragraph. Make these specific to the industry — not generic AP complexity.

**Key Pain Points Callout Box** (light blue background)
6–7 bullets formatted as discovery questions or pain statements specific to this sector.

**Technology Landscape**
Intro sentence, then a 2-column table: Technology Layer | Common Platforms in This Sector
Rows: ERP, Procurement/Sourcing, AP Automation, Payments, Supplier Portals
Include a paragraph after explaining where Vic.ai fits ("above the ERP" positioning).

**Digital Transformation Trends: [Current Year]–[Current Year+1]**
5–6 bullet points specific to this industry's current transformation initiatives.

**Conversation Starters**
Callout box with 6–7 discovery questions phrased as direct quotes ("How are you...?") specific to this industry.

**Why Vic.ai Resonates in [Industry]**
2-column table: Sector Challenge | Vic.ai Response (6–8 rows, all specific to the industry).

## 2. Role-Based Outreach

This output has two modes depending on what the rep provides:

**MODE A — Industry only (no specific company)**
- Header: "ROLE-BASED OUTREACH | [Industry] | Vic.ai Sales Intelligence"
- Subtitle: "General Outreach Templates by Persona — [Industry]"
- All messaging uses industry-level hooks (trends, pressures, typical complexity) rather than company-specific facts
- No contact names — describe the typical profile, priorities, and pain for each role in that industry
- Emails and LinkedIn messages should feel relevant to anyone in that role at a company in that sector

**MODE B — Specific company provided**
- Header: "ROLE-BASED OUTREACH | [Company Name] | Vic.ai Sales Intelligence"
- Subtitle: "Tailored Emails and LinkedIn Messages by Persona"
- Use real contact names where found in research; if a name isn't findable for a role, note "Name not publicly available" and write messaging for that title generically
- All hooks, subject lines, and body copy should reference specific company facts (recent news, acquisitions, strategic signals, known tech stack)

### The 4 Personas (always include all 4, in this order):

**Persona 1: CFO / Chief Financial Officer**
**Persona 2: Controller / Head of Accounting**
**Persona 3: AP Director / AP Manager**
**Persona 4: VP of Finance**

---

### For each persona, include:

**Persona heading:** "Persona [N]: [Role Title]"
**Role tag line:** "[Name if known, or 'Role: [Title]'] — [Company or Industry]"
**Background paragraph:** 2–3 sentences on this person's priorities, what they own day-to-day, and what AP-related pain they feel most acutely. In Mode A, describe the typical profile in this industry. In Mode B, reference actual background if found.

**Cold Email** (message box with light blue background):
- Subject line (specific and relevant — not generic)
- 4–5 short paragraphs, 150–200 words total
- Opening: In Mode A, open with an industry trend or pressure. In Mode B, open with a specific company fact, recent news, or strategic signal.
- Body: Validate their operational complexity from the perspective of their specific role (CFO cares about cost and close speed; Controller cares about accuracy and exceptions; AP Director cares about team capacity and vendor relationships; VP Finance cares about visibility and working capital)
- Value: One specific Vic.ai outcome framed in their language
- CTA: Soft ask for 20 minutes — no hard sell

**LinkedIn Message** (message box):
- 3–4 sentences max
- In Mode A: open with an industry insight or pressure relevant to their role
- In Mode B: open with a specific company fact or news hook
- Asks one diagnostic question or offers a relevant insight
- No pitch — invite a response

**Why This Works** (amber/gold callout box):
3–4 bullets explaining the strategic choices in the messaging — why the hook works for this specific role, what objection it pre-empts, and why the CTA is calibrated to their seniority level.

## 3. Full Account Sales Brief

**Subtitle line:** "[Company Name] — Full Account Sales Brief"
**Header:** "ACCOUNT SALES BRIEF | [Company Name] | Vic.ai Sales Intelligence"

### Stats header (3-column dark cards, 2 rows):
Row 1: Est. Revenue | Employees | Primary Industry
Row 2: [Key operational metric — e.g., # locations, # brands, # acquisitions] | Ownership | HQ

### Sections (in order):

**Company Snapshot**
2-column snapshot table with rows:
Founded, HQ, Ownership, Industry, Products/Services, Key Brands (if applicable), Markets Served, Distribution Model, Geographic Presence

**Key Decision Makers**
2-column table: Name/Title | Relevance to Vic.ai (4–6 rows, use real names where found)

**AP Complexity Assessment**
Intro paragraph on overall complexity level, then:
- "Complexity Drivers" callout box: 5–7 specific bullets about what makes their AP hard

**Technology & ERP Landscape**
Intro sentence + 2-column table: Layer | Known/Likely Platform
(ERP, Procurement, AP Automation, Payments, Supplier Portals)
Then a "Vic.ai Positioning" callout box (green background): 4 bullets on how Vic.ai fits their specific stack situation.

**Strategic Signals & Timing**
2-column table: Signal | What It Means for Vic.ai (5–7 rows with specific recent signals)

**Talking Points & Discovery Questions**
- "Opening Talking Points" sub-heading: 3 bullets (specific to this company)
- "Discovery Questions" sub-heading: 5 questions (specific to their situation)

**Objection Handling**
2-column table: Objection | Response (5 rows — use standard Vic.ai objections but tailor responses to this company's context)

**Sample Outreach Frame**
Callout box with:
- Target persona and name
- Email hook/subject suggestion
- 3-bullet outline of the email structure
- CTA recommendation

**Competitive Context**
Intro sentence + 2-column table: Competitor | Vic.ai Differentiation (5–6 rows)

**Finance Team Hiring Signals**
Only include this section if relevant open roles were found in research. Intro sentence explaining why hiring signals matter (e.g. open AP roles signal team strain; new finance leadership signals transformation appetite). Then:
- A 2-column table: Role Being Hired | What It Signals for Vic.ai (3–5 rows max)
- A callout box if there is a strong signal worth highlighting (e.g. "Actively hiring 3 AP Specialists suggests current team is at capacity — strong timing signal for automation conversation")
- If no relevant roles found, omit this section entirely (do not include a placeholder)

## 4. Executive One-Pager

**Purpose:** This is a presentation-ready business case document — not a data sheet, and not intended to be sent to the prospect directly. It's designed to be used by a rep in a meeting, as a leave-behind after a discovery call, or to prepare a manager or champion internally. The framing should connect the prospect's specific company/industry situation directly to Vic.ai's impact and value — every section should answer "so what does this mean for them?"

**Design philosophy:** Visual, scannable, and narrative-driven. Use large bold section labels, generous spacing, and colored highlight blocks. Minimize raw data tables — favor insight statements over lists of facts. Should feel like a polished business presentation, not a research brief.

**Header:** No running header (title block serves this purpose).

### Layout (top to bottom):

**Title Banner** (full-width dark block):
Large: "[Company Name]"
Sub: "Business Case Overview | Vic.ai"
Right-aligned in same block: Industry | Date

**3 Headline Stats** (1 row of 3 large accent-colored cards — pick the 3 most impactful numbers):
Examples: Revenue, # of Locations/Plants, # of Acquisitions (or Employees if more relevant)
Each card: large bold number + short label underneath

**Industry Pressures** (full-width section, light blue background):
Label: "WHAT'S DRIVING CHANGE IN [INDUSTRY]"
3–4 pressure statements, each as a bold headline + 1-sentence explanation. These are industry-level forces (regulatory change, M&A consolidation, margin pressure, labor costs, etc.) that make AP automation increasingly urgent in this sector. Frame these as market realities, not Vic.ai claims.

**Their Situation** (full-width or 2-column section):
Label: "THE [COMPANY NAME] ENVIRONMENT"
3–4 company-specific statements that describe the operational complexity driving AP pain. Each should be a bold insight + 1 sentence of context. Draw from what was researched: acquisitions, multi-site operations, ERP fragmentation, international expansion, growth trajectory, etc.
Frame as observations, not accusations: "Operating across X locations with Y acquired brands creates natural AP complexity" not "Your AP is broken."

**The Vic.ai Impact** (full-width, green-tinted background):
Label: "WHERE VIC.AI CREATES VALUE"
A 2-column table (no header row — just alternating shading):
Left: Their specific challenge (tied to "Their Situation" above)
Right: The Vic.ai outcome, framed as a result ("Invoice processing time reduced by X%", "Closes books faster", "AP team capacity freed for strategic work")
4–5 rows. Make each right-side cell feel like a concrete business outcome, not a feature description.

**Why Now** (full-width):
Label: "WHY THIS MATTERS NOW"
3 signal cards (amber / green / accent blue backgrounds) in a row:
Each card: short bold signal title + 1–2 sentence explanation of why this creates urgency for Vic.ai specifically.
Signals should be specific to the company's current situation (recent acquisition, new leadership, PE pressure, hiring strain, etc.)

**Footer:** "Prepared by Vic.ai Sales" + date, in small gray italic. Sources line below that.

## Shared Formatting Patterns

### Callout Box (light blue)
Table, 1 column, full width (9360 DXA), LIGHT_BG fill, bold title in ACCENT color, bullet list below.

### Signal Card (colored background)
Small table cell with colored background (AMBER_BG, GREEN_BG, or LIGHT_BG), mini bold heading + 1–2 sentence body.

### Term Table
3-column: Term (bold, MED_BG or LIGHT_BG alternating) | Definition | "Why it matters" (italic, gray).
Column widths: 2400 | 3800 | 3160 DXA.

### 2-Column Data Table
Header row in ACCENT (white bold text). Alternating WHITE/LIGHT_BG rows. Column widths vary by content (set explicitly, always sum to 9360 DXA).

### Page Break
Always use `new Paragraph({ children: [new PageBreak()] })` — never a standalone PageBreak.

### Lists
Always use `numbering: { reference: "bullets", level: 0 }` — never unicode bullet characters.

### Cell Margins
Always include: `margins: { top: 80, bottom: 80, left: 120, right: 120 }` on all table cells.

### Shading
Always use `ShadingType.CLEAR` — never `ShadingType.SOLID`.
